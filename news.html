Photo of the contract ceremony. Professor Dongsoo Han (right) of the Department of Computer Science


Department of Computer Science and Engineering Professor Dongsoo Han’s team, signed MOUs with Orange Shock Co., Ltd. and Visoft Co., Ltd.

A research team led by Professor Dongsoo Han of the Department of Computer Science at our university has signed a strategic business agreement (MOU) with Orange Shock (CEO Taeyoon Koh) and V.Isoft Co., Ltd. on the establishment of an extended reality (XR) digital IP platform linked to NFT and Metaverse.
The signing ceremony, held at the Mondrian Hotel in Itaewon-dong, Yongsan-gu, Seoul on the 28th of last month, was held in the presence of major executives and employees of the three companies, including Professor Dong-soo Han, head of the Exhibition Technology Research Center at KAIST Smart Science Center, and Hyuk-soon Kwon, Vice President of V.Isoft.
With the agreement on this day, Orange Shock's CEO Taeyoon Ko mixed and expanded various exhibition technologies related to indoor location-based AR/VR, which KAIST is researching and developing, and the AR technology called 'Snap-knock' provided by V.Isoft Co., Ltd. He explained that he was going to build a reality (MR·XR) digital IP platform.
Professor Dongsoo Han, head of the exhibition technology division at KAIST Smart Science Center, said, “The mixed/extended reality (MR·XR) field is expected to grow rapidly as a high value-added industry in the future. I am very pleased to be able to build a mixed/extended reality (MR·XR) connected with NFT·Metaverse by combining the location-based display technology possessed by the company and VR·AR technology of V.Isoft Co., Ltd.” .
Through this agreement, Orange Shock Co., Ltd. promised to support related businesses and alliances for the metaverse and NFT combination with KAIST and VL Soft, and plans to promote B2B business through the channels owned by the three companies.
